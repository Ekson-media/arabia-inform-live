Our Story

Built to Preserve a Civilization's Voice

Arabia Inform was established in January 1998 with a mission that had never been attempted at scale: to build a living, growing, permanent digital memory of the Arabic world's intellectual and media output.

At a time when much of the Arab region's media existed only in print, on broadcast airwaves, or in institutional filing systems, Arabia Inform began the monumental task of digitizing, structuring, and preserving this knowledge for future generations.

For over 27 years, without a single day of interruption, the company has operated two dedicated centers ‚Äî one focused on content acquisition and digitization, the other on classification, indexing, and bibliographic production. Together, they form the most comprehensive Arabic knowledge infrastructure ever built.

Today, Arabia Inform's archive is not simply a library ‚Äî it is a living, searchable, AI-ready corpus of Arabic civilization's documented thought: every article, every broadcast, every dissertation, every book of significance, meticulously preserved and organized.

Our Journey

27 Years of the Arabic Memory

Every milestone below represents thousands of hours of dedicated work, growing an archive that has never missed a single day.

1997

Arabia Inform founded. Mission defined: build the complete Arabic digital memory. Initial infrastructure and operational model established.

Jan 1998

Operations begin. First newspapers digitized and indexed. 24/7 continuous operation commences ‚Äî the Arabic Memory starts growing.

2000

Archive reaches first major milestone. Coverage expands systematically across all 22 Arab countries. Print media indexing reaches full scale.

2004

Online media archiving begins. Systematic web crawling of Arabic news sites initiated. The digital expansion of the archive takes shape.

2006

Broadcast archive established. TV and radio recording, digitization, and clipping begins. 3,360+ channels now continuously monitored.

2009

YouTube channel archiving added to the ecosystem. 14,000+ channels now systematically captured and catalogued.

2010s

Academic archive expansion accelerates. Dissertations, journals, and conference papers systematically indexed to international bibliographic standards.

2015+

Full-text conversion initiative launched. Image-based print content converted to machine-readable, searchable text at scale.

2020+

AI-readiness initiative. Archive restructured and annotated for NLP and machine learning applications. The foundation for Arabic AI built.

2024

Archive exceeds 3 billion online articles. 280M+ print clips. 120M+ broadcast hours. The largest structured Arabic corpus on earth.

Today

27+ years. Operating continuously. Growing every hour of every day. 3.28+ billion items. Zero days missed. The Arabic Memory lives.
